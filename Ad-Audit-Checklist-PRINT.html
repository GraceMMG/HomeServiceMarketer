Audit Section 1 of 4
Google Ads Audit
20 checkpoints to find wasted spend, fix underperforming campaigns, and make every dollar work harder.
Before You Start

Open your Google Ads account in one window and work through each checkpoint. For items requiring data, look at the last 30–90 days of performance. Score 1 point for each item you can confirm is set up correctly right now.

Tracking & Foundation

  • Checkpoint 1
    Conversion tracking is active and verified
    In Google Ads, go to Tools → Conversions. Confirm at least one conversion action (phone call, form submission, or booking) shows "Recording conversions" — not "No recent conversions" or "Unverified."
    Why it matters: Without active conversion tracking, Google's Smart Bidding has zero data to optimize toward. You're paying for clicks with no idea which ones become real leads.
  • Checkpoint 2
    Phone call conversions are tracked (not just form fills)
    Most home service leads come through phone calls. Confirm that calls from ads (Google forwarding number) and/or calls from your website are being tracked as conversions.
    Why it matters: If you're only tracking form fills, you're missing 60–80% of your actual conversions. Your campaigns look underperforming when they're actually generating calls.
  • Checkpoint 3
    Google Ads is linked to Google Analytics
    In Google Ads, go to Tools → Linked accounts and confirm Google Analytics is connected. This enables cross-channel attribution and richer audience data.
    Why it matters: Linked accounts let you see what happens after the click — bounce rate, pages visited, time on site — so you can diagnose whether your landing pages are working.
  • Checkpoint 4
    Each campaign has a clear, single objective
    Each campaign should have one goal: emergency calls, inspection requests, replacement estimates, or maintenance bookings. Campaigns mixing objectives dilute budget and reporting clarity.
    Why it matters: Focused campaigns let you set appropriate bids, write matching ad copy, and send traffic to the right landing page — all of which directly improve conversion rates.

Keywords & Match Types

  • Checkpoint 5
    You are NOT relying solely on broad match keywords
    Confirm your keyword list includes a mix of phrase match and exact match keywords. Broad match alone on limited budgets causes Google to show your ads for loosely related — often irrelevant — searches.
    Why it matters: Broad match on "roofing" can trigger your ad for "roofing nails," "roofing books," or "roofing jobs near me." Wasted clicks add up to hundreds or thousands of dollars per month.
  • Checkpoint 6
    Search Terms report reviewed in the last 30 days
    Go to Keywords → Search Terms. Review what actual searches triggered your ads. Look for irrelevant queries that spent budget without converting.
    Why it matters: The Search Terms report is where you find wasted spend hiding in plain sight. Most contractors who review this for the first time discover 20–40% of their budget is going to completely irrelevant searches.
  • Checkpoint 7
    A negative keyword list exists with at least 20 terms
    Go to Keywords → Negative Keywords. Confirm you have a campaign-level or account-level negative keyword list that includes terms like: "jobs," "careers," "hiring," "DIY," "training," "how to," "free," "YouTube," "Reddit."
    Why it matters: Negative keywords are free money. Every irrelevant click you block is a dollar saved that can go toward reaching a real buyer instead.
  • Checkpoint 8
    Quality Scores are 6 or higher on primary keywords
    Check Quality Score by clicking the columns icon and adding the QS column to your keyword view. Primary money keywords should score 6–10. Scores of 3–5 indicate misalignment between keyword, ad copy, and landing page.
    Why it matters: A Quality Score of 10 vs. 4 on the same keyword can mean paying 2–3x more per click. Higher QS = lower CPC = more clicks for the same budget.
  • Checkpoint 9
    Location-specific keywords included (e.g., "[service] [city]")
    Confirm your keyword list includes geo-modified terms: "roof replacement Dallas," "AC repair near me," "HVAC contractor [city name]." These typically have higher intent and lower competition than generic terms.
    Why it matters: Local search terms convert at a significantly higher rate than generic terms. Homeowners searching "[service] [city]" are ready to call, not just browsing.

Ad Copy & Extensions

  • Checkpoint 10
    Responsive Search Ads (RSAs) used — not old Expanded Text Ads
    Each ad group should contain at least one RSA with 10–15 headlines and 3–4 descriptions. Expanded Text Ads are no longer supported for new creation and underperform RSAs significantly.
    Why it matters: RSAs allow Google to test headline and description combinations automatically, showing the highest-performing combination to each user. Better ad relevance = higher CTR = lower CPCs.
  • Checkpoint 11
    Call extensions (phone number) enabled on all campaigns
    Go to Ads & Extensions → Extensions → Call Extensions. Confirm a call extension with your correct phone number is attached to all active campaigns. Set preferred call time to match your business hours.
    Why it matters: A call extension lets mobile users call you directly from the search results without clicking through to your site. For home services, this is one of the highest-converting ad elements available.
  • Checkpoint 12
    Sitelink extensions added (at least 4 sitelinks)
    Sitelinks expand your ad's footprint and link to specific pages: "Free Estimate," "Emergency Service," "Our Reviews," "Financing Options." Confirm at least 4 active sitelinks per campaign.
    Why it matters: Ads with sitelinks get significantly more clicks and take up more visual space on the search results page — effectively blocking competitor ads from appearing above the fold.
  • Checkpoint 13
    Ad headlines include primary keywords and location
    Review your RSA headlines. At minimum, include: the primary service keyword, your city/area, and a compelling differentiator ("Licensed & Insured," "Same-Day Service," "Free Estimates").
    Why it matters: Google bolds keywords in headlines that match the user's search query. Keyword-matching headlines dramatically improve click-through rates and Quality Scores.

Bidding, Budget & Targeting

  • Checkpoint 14
    No campaigns show "Limited by budget" status
    In your Campaigns view, check the Status column. If any campaign says "Eligible (Limited)" or "Limited by budget," you are missing impressions for searches you're already willing to pay for.
    Why it matters: If your best campaigns are budget-limited, you're capping your lead volume artificially. Either increase budget or pause lower-priority campaigns to redirect spend to what's converting.
  • Checkpoint 15
    Bidding strategy matches campaign maturity and data volume
    New campaigns (<30 conversions/month): use Maximize Clicks or Manual CPC to gather data. Established campaigns (30+ conversions/month): use Target CPA or Maximize Conversions to let Smart Bidding optimize.
    Why it matters: Using Smart Bidding before you have enough conversion data causes Google to optimize toward statistical noise, often inflating CPCs and reducing lead quality.
  • Checkpoint 16
    Location targeting set to "Presence" not "Presence or interest"
    In Campaign Settings → Locations, set targeting to "Presence: People in or regularly in your targeted locations." The default "Presence or interest" wastes budget on people who have searched for your area but don't live there.
    Why it matters: The "interest" setting can show your ads to people anywhere in the country who once searched for your city. For local home services, this is almost always pure wasted spend.
  • Checkpoint 17
    Ad schedule set to your actual operating hours
    Go to Campaign Settings → Ad Schedule. If you don't answer phones 24/7, restrict ads to hours when someone can actually reach you. Or if you do offer 24/7 response, confirm ads are running around the clock.
    Why it matters: Paying for clicks at 2 AM when no one answers the phone wastes budget and trains Google's algorithm with lower conversion data during those hours.
  • Checkpoint 18
    Search Impression Share above 40% on primary campaigns
    Add the "Search Impr. Share" column to your Campaigns view. If your primary revenue campaigns are below 40% impression share, you're losing more than half the available searches to competitors or budget constraints.
    Why it matters: Low impression share means your competitors are capturing leads that should be yours. Improving it means more opportunities without necessarily raising your max CPC.

Landing Pages

  • Checkpoint 19
    Each ad campaign sends traffic to a matching landing page
    Your "Emergency AC Repair" ad should not send traffic to your homepage. Confirm each campaign's ads link to a page specifically about that service, with a matching headline, offer, and CTA.
    Why it matters: Message match between ad and landing page is the single biggest lever for improving conversion rate. A 2% → 5% landing page conversion rate is worth more than halving your CPC.
  • Checkpoint 20
    Landing pages load in under 3 seconds on mobile
    Test your landing page URLs at PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev). Check the Mobile score. A score below 50 or a load time above 3 seconds is killing your conversion rate and increasing your CPCs.
    Why it matters: Google uses landing page experience as part of Quality Score. Slow pages lower QS, which raises your CPCs. And 53% of mobile users abandon a page that takes more than 3 seconds to load.

Audit Section 2 of 4
Facebook & Instagram Ads Audit
20 checkpoints covering campaign setup, audience targeting, creative performance, Pixel health, and retargeting.
Before You Start

Open Meta Business Suite and Meta Ads Manager. Have your Events Manager open to check Pixel status. Score 1 point for each item confirmed as correctly set up right now.

Pixel & Tracking Foundation

  • Checkpoint 21
    Meta Pixel is installed and firing on your website
    In Meta Events Manager, confirm your Pixel shows "Active" status with recent activity. Install the "Meta Pixel Helper" Chrome extension to test it directly on your website — it will show you exactly what events are firing.
    Why it matters: Without the Pixel, you cannot run retargeting campaigns, build lookalike audiences, or track website conversions from Facebook ads. It is the foundation of all advanced Facebook advertising.
  • Checkpoint 22
    Conversion events are configured (Lead, Contact, or Schedule)
    In Events Manager, confirm that "Lead," "Contact," or a custom conversion event fires when someone submits a form or calls from your site. Standard Pixel page view tracking alone is not sufficient for optimization.
    Why it matters: Without conversion events, you can't use conversion-optimized campaign objectives, and Facebook can't learn which audiences are more likely to become leads for your business.
  • Checkpoint 23
    UTM parameters added to all ad destination URLs
    Check your ad destination URLs — they should include UTM tags like ?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=free-inspection. Use Google's UTM builder to create them if missing.
    Why it matters: UTM parameters let you see in Google Analytics which specific Facebook campaigns, ad sets, and ads are driving traffic and conversions — not just that "social media" sent visitors.

Campaign Structure & Objectives

  • Checkpoint 24
    Lead generation campaigns use "Leads" objective — not "Traffic"
    In Ads Manager, check your active campaigns' objectives. Campaigns intended to generate leads should use the "Leads" objective (which optimizes for form fills or calls) — not "Traffic," "Reach," or "Awareness."
    Why it matters: The campaign objective tells Facebook's algorithm what to optimize for. Traffic campaigns optimize for clicks — often from people who will never hire you. Leads campaigns optimize for actual lead conversions.
  • Checkpoint 25
    Campaigns have been running at least 14 days before judging
    Check the date range on your reporting. If you've paused or significantly changed campaigns before the 14-day learning period ends, you've been resetting the algorithm's optimization repeatedly.
    Why it matters: Facebook's algorithm needs time to find the right people within your audience. Changing budgets, targeting, or creatives frequently restarts the learning phase and prevents campaigns from reaching their full performance potential.
  • Checkpoint 26
    Daily budget is at least $20/day per active ad set
    Check each ad set's daily budget. Ad sets running on $5–$10/day don't gather enough data for the algorithm to optimize effectively, especially in competitive home service markets.
    Why it matters: Meta's algorithm needs approximately 50 conversion events per week to exit the learning phase. At $5/day with $30 CPLs, that's mathematically impossible. Underfunded ad sets stay stuck in "Learning Limited" indefinitely.

Audience Targeting

  • Checkpoint 27
    Audience size is between 100,000 and 2,000,000
    Check each ad set's audience size indicator. The "too narrow" zone (under 50K) limits delivery. The "too broad" zone (over 5M in a local market) wastes budget on people outside your service area.
    Why it matters: The sweet spot gives the algorithm enough people to find your best prospects while keeping the audience relevant to local homeowners who can actually hire you.
  • Checkpoint 28
    Homeowner behaviors or interests included in targeting
    Confirm your ad sets include at least some homeowner-relevant targeting: interests like "home improvement," "homeownership," or behaviors like "likely homeowner" (where available in your country's ad platform).
    Why it matters: Showing roofing or HVAC ads to renters is wasted spend. Layering homeowner signals, even loosely, meaningfully improves lead quality and cost per lead.
  • Checkpoint 29
    A Lookalike Audience has been created from past customers
    In Audiences, confirm you have a Lookalike Audience built from a customer list, past lead list, or your website converters. A 1–2% lookalike of your [City] service area is a highly effective audience for home services.
    Why it matters: Lookalike audiences let Facebook find people who statistically resemble your best customers. They typically outperform interest-based audiences significantly on cost per lead.
  • Checkpoint 30
    Ad frequency is below 3.5 for campaigns running over 2 weeks
    Add the "Frequency" column to your Ads Manager view. If frequency exceeds 3–4 on the same audience over 2 weeks, performance will drop as the same people keep seeing the same ad.
    Why it matters: Ad fatigue is real. When the same person sees your ad 5–7 times without acting, they start to associate your brand with annoyance rather than value. Rotate creative or expand the audience.

Creative & Retargeting

  • Checkpoint 31
    At least 3 different creative variants tested per ad set
    Each ad set should contain 3+ ad variations testing different images, headlines, or primary text. Check the "Creative" level breakdown within each ad set to confirm variety exists.
    Why it matters: You cannot know which creative resonates until you test. Running a single ad means leaving performance improvements on the table. Even swapping the hero image can cut CPL in half.
  • Checkpoint 32
    Before/after photo creative has been tested
    Confirm at least one active or recently tested ad uses a real before/after job photo as the creative. This is consistently the top-performing creative format for home service businesses.
    Why it matters: Before/after images communicate transformation instantly — no one needs to read a word to understand what you do. They stop the scroll and create the "I need that" reaction in homeowners.
  • Checkpoint 33
    Video creative has been tested (at least 15–30 seconds)
    Confirm at least one video ad has been tested — a job walk-through, customer testimonial, or time-lapse of a completed project. Video does not need to be professionally produced to perform.
    Why it matters: Video ads typically have lower CPMs (cost per 1,000 views) and build brand familiarity faster than static images. Even a 20-second iPhone video of a completed job can outperform polished stock-photo ads.
  • Checkpoint 34
    A website retargeting campaign is active
    In Ads Manager, confirm there is a separate campaign targeting "Website Visitors — last 30 days" (or 60 days). This requires the Meta Pixel to be installed and collecting data.
    Why it matters: People who visited your website are 5–10x more likely to convert than cold audiences. Retargeting them keeps you top-of-mind at the moment they're actively comparing options — often the highest-ROI ad spend available.
  • Checkpoint 35
    Lead forms are short — 3 fields maximum
    If using Facebook Lead Ads (instant forms), confirm the form asks for name, phone number, and optionally email — nothing more. Every additional field reduces completion rates.
    Why it matters: Lead form completion rates drop dramatically with each additional field. A 3-field form converts at 2–3x the rate of a 7-field form. Get their phone number and call them — gather the rest verbally.
  • Checkpoint 36
    Cost per lead (CPL) is tracked and compared to a benchmark
    Confirm you know your actual CPL from Facebook ads over the last 30–90 days. Home services benchmarks: HVAC $25–$55, Roofing $30–$70, Plumbing $20–$45, Landscaping $15–$35. Are you above, at, or below these ranges?
    Why it matters: Without tracking CPL, you have no objective measure of whether your campaigns are performing well or poorly. A rising CPL is an early warning signal — usually pointing to audience fatigue or creative decay.
  • Checkpoint 37
    Seasonal or event-triggered campaigns are planned in advance
    Confirm you have a process (even a calendar reminder) to adjust Facebook campaigns before seasonal peaks — spring/summer AC season, fall furnace tune-up season, pre-storm season for roofing.
    Why it matters: Campaigns take 7–14 days to exit the learning phase. Launching a summer AC campaign on the first hot day of the year means you miss the peak. Proactive planning captures peak-season leads at lower CPLs before competitors flood the auction.
  • Checkpoint 38
    Page engagement audience used for warm retargeting
    Create a Custom Audience of "People who engaged with your Facebook Page" in the last 180 days. This includes people who liked a post, watched a video, or clicked on an ad — all warmer than cold audiences.
    Why it matters: Page engagers have already shown interest in your business. Retargeting them with a direct offer typically yields CPLs 30–50% lower than cold targeting and leverages the trust built by your organic content.
  • Checkpoint 39
    Underperforming ads (CTR below 0.8%) paused or replaced
    Sort your ads by CTR. Any ad running for 7+ days with a CTR below 0.8% is underperforming for home services. Pause these and reallocate budget to ads with CTR above 1.2%.
    Why it matters: Low-CTR ads drag down ad set performance and increase average CPMs. Facebook rewards ads that users engage with — pausing poor performers allows budget to flow to what's actually working.
  • Checkpoint 40
    New leads from Facebook ads are followed up within 5 minutes
    Confirm your process for Facebook leads (instant form or website form). Is there an automated text or email response? Does someone call within 5 minutes during business hours? Check your CRM or ad notification settings.
    Why it matters: Research shows lead contact rates drop by 80% after the first 5 minutes. A great ad with a slow follow-up process throws money away at the finish line. Speed-to-lead is as important as cost-per-lead.

Audit Section 3 of 4
Google Business Profile Audit
15 checkpoints to maximize your visibility in local search and the Google Map Pack — where 60–80% of local service clicks happen.

Profile Completeness

  • Checkpoint 41
    Profile is claimed, verified, and shows "Verified" status
    Go to business.google.com and confirm your profile shows a blue checkmark and "Verified" status. An unverified profile cannot rank properly and is vulnerable to hijacking by competitors.
    Why it matters: Unverified profiles are filtered lower in local search results. Worse, anyone can suggest edits to an unverified listing — including competitors who may change your phone number or address.
  • Checkpoint 42
    Primary business category is the most specific trade category
    Check your primary category in GBP settings. It should be the most specific option for your primary service: "Roofing Contractor," "HVAC Contractor," "Plumber," "Landscaper" — not a generic category like "Contractor" or "Home Services."
    Why it matters: Google's primary category is the single most heavily weighted ranking signal for the Local Pack. Using a generic category means you won't appear for high-intent searches like "roofer near me" or "AC repair."
  • Checkpoint 43
    All secondary categories are added
    Add every relevant secondary category: if you're a roofer, add "Gutter Contractor," "Siding Contractor," "Handyman." If HVAC, add "Air Conditioning Contractor," "Heating Contractor," "Insulation Contractor."
    Why it matters: Secondary categories let you appear in search results for related services without diluting your primary category. Each additional relevant category is a free opportunity to rank for more searches.
  • Checkpoint 44
    Business description is 500–750 characters and keyword-rich
    Read your current GBP description. It should include: your primary service, your city and service area cities, years in business, license number, and a differentiator. Aim for 500–750 characters (under the 750-character limit).
    Why it matters: The description is indexed by Google and helps match your profile to search queries. A description that mentions "[City] roofing contractor" will rank for that phrase; a generic description will not.
  • Checkpoint 45
    All services are listed with individual descriptions
    In GBP, go to Services and confirm every service you offer is listed — each with a short description (1–2 sentences) that naturally includes the service name and your city. Don't leave descriptions blank.
    Why it matters: Google displays your services in your profile and uses them as ranking signals. Customers can also filter searches by service type — if your service isn't listed, you won't appear.
  • Checkpoint 46
    Service area includes all cities and zip codes you actually serve
    Go to GBP Settings → Business Location. For service-area businesses, confirm you've added every city, town, and major zip code you serve. You can add up to 20 service areas.
    Why it matters: Your profile only appears in searches from locations included in your service area. If you serve 10 cities but only listed 2, you're invisible to 80% of your potential local market on Google Maps.

Photos & Posts

  • Checkpoint 47
    Profile has at least 25 photos (ideally 50+)
    Check your photo count in GBP Photos. Count both your uploaded photos and any customer photos. Profiles with more photos consistently rank higher and receive more profile clicks than sparse profiles.
    Why it matters: Google's own data shows profiles with 100+ photos receive 520% more calls than profiles with no photos. Photos are a ranking signal AND a conversion signal — more photos = more calls.
  • Checkpoint 48
    New photos uploaded within the last 30 days
    Check the date of your most recent photo upload. If it's been more than 30 days, upload at least 5 new photos today — job photos, team shots, or completed work.
    Why it matters: Google uses recency signals to assess business activity. Profiles with regular new content (photos, posts) appear more relevant to recent searches than dormant profiles. Fresh photos signal an active business.
  • Checkpoint 49
    A Google Post has been published within the last 7 days
    Go to GBP → Posts and check when the last post was published. Posts expire after 7 days. Active profiles with regular posts outperform dormant ones in local rankings.
    Why it matters: Google Posts appear directly in your listing in search results — essentially free ad space below your business info. Regular posts keep your profile fresh and give searchers a reason to click over a competitor.

Reviews & Engagement

  • Checkpoint 50
    Profile has at least 25 Google reviews with 4.5+ average rating
    Check your review count and average rating on your GBP dashboard. Below 25 reviews and you're largely invisible in competitive local markets. Below 4.5 stars and conversion rates drop significantly.
    Why it matters: Review count is a major GBP ranking factor. More importantly, customers use review count as a trust proxy — 150 reviews at 4.7 stars will get clicked over 12 reviews at 5.0 stars almost every time.
  • Checkpoint 51
    Every review has been responded to (including negatives)
    Filter your reviews and check for any without a response. Every review — 5 stars and 1 star — deserves a personalized response, not a generic template.
    Why it matters: Responding to reviews is a GBP ranking signal AND a public display of customer service. Future customers read your responses to negative reviews closely — how you handle complaints tells them more about your business than the 5-star reviews do.
  • Checkpoint 52
    A system exists to request reviews from every completed job
    Confirm you have a repeatable process — text template, CRM automation, or verbal script — to ask every customer for a Google review within 24 hours of job completion.
    Why it matters: Review velocity (how fast you're accumulating new reviews) is a ranking signal. A competitor gaining 10 reviews/month will outrank a contractor with better overall ratings but only 1 new review/month.
  • Checkpoint 53
    Q&A section has at least 4 questions pre-populated with answers
    Go to your GBP listing and scroll to Q&A. Add common questions customers ask before hiring you — about licensing, insurance, pricing, process, and guarantees — and answer each one thoroughly.
    Why it matters: If you don't populate Q&A, anyone can submit questions — and strangers may answer them inaccurately. Pre-populating your Q&A controls the narrative and reduces friction for customers on the fence.
  • Checkpoint 54
    Business hours are accurate and updated for upcoming holidays
    Verify your current hours in GBP. Set holiday hours in advance (Google will prompt you). If you offer emergency/24-7 service, note it in your business description even if your regular hours are 8–5.
    Why it matters: Google displays "Closed" warnings when hours are inaccurate. A customer calling during listed hours who gets no answer will leave a negative review or choose a competitor — and your GBP hours may be the reason they called at the wrong time.
  • Checkpoint 55
    Website link in GBP points to a relevant service page (not just the homepage)
    Check the website URL listed in your GBP profile. Ideally it should link to a location-specific landing page or your most relevant service page — not just yourcompany.com.
    Why it matters: A customer clicking from your GBP expecting to hire an HVAC contractor should land on an HVAC-specific page — not a generic homepage. Better landing page relevance means higher conversion rates from GBP traffic.

Audit Section 4 of 4
Website Conversion Audit
15 checkpoints to find why visitors are leaving your site without becoming leads — and what to fix first.
Before You Start

Open your website on a phone (not a desktop) and work through these checkpoints from the perspective of a homeowner seeing it for the first time. Also have Google Analytics and your site's backend open. Score 1 point per confirmed item.

Speed & Technical Foundation

  • Checkpoint 56
    Website loads in under 3 seconds on mobile
    Test at PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev) with your homepage URL. Check the Mobile tab. The "First Contentful Paint" metric should be under 2.5 seconds. If it's above 4 seconds, this is an urgent fix.
    Why it matters: 53% of mobile users abandon a website that takes more than 3 seconds to load. Every second of delay reduces conversions by approximately 7%. Slow sites also rank lower in Google search results.
  • Checkpoint 57
    Website is fully mobile-responsive
    View your homepage on a smartphone. All text should be readable without zooming, buttons should be tappable, forms should be easy to complete, and nothing should be cut off horizontally.
    Why it matters: Over 65% of home service website traffic now comes from mobile devices. A site that's hard to use on a phone loses the majority of its potential leads before they ever see your phone number.
  • Checkpoint 58
    SSL certificate installed — site uses HTTPS not HTTP
    Check the browser address bar for your website. It should show a padlock icon and begin with "https://" — not "http://." Sites without SSL are flagged "Not Secure" by browsers, which kills trust instantly.
    Why it matters: Google uses HTTPS as a ranking factor. More importantly, visitors who see a "Not Secure" warning on their phone leave immediately — especially when filling out contact forms with their phone number and address.

Contact & Calls to Action

  • Checkpoint 59
    Phone number is visible in the header on every page
    Check every page of your site on mobile. Your phone number should appear in the navigation header at the top, in large enough text to read at a glance — without scrolling or searching.
    Why it matters: Your phone number is your primary call to action. If a homeowner has to hunt for it, they won't. They'll go back to Google and call the competitor whose number is right there at the top.
  • Checkpoint 60
    Phone number is a click-to-call link on mobile
    On your phone, tap the phone number on your website. It should immediately prompt to initiate a phone call. If tapping the number does nothing, it's not coded as a click-to-call link — a significant conversion killer for mobile users.
    Why it matters: Every extra tap between a lead and a phone call reduces conversions. A non-tappable phone number on mobile means the lead has to manually dial — most won't bother, especially if a competitor's site makes it one tap.
  • Checkpoint 61
    A contact form appears above the fold on the homepage
    On your homepage (desktop and mobile), check if a contact form — or a prominent button to a contact form — is visible without scrolling. "Above the fold" means visible the instant the page loads.
    Why it matters: Many homeowners prefer to fill out a form over calling, especially late at night when they notice a problem. If your form is buried, you lose after-hours leads who represent 30–40% of home service inquiry volume.
  • Checkpoint 62
    Every page has exactly one primary CTA — not three competing options
    Review each page. The primary call to action ("Get a Free Estimate," "Call Now," "Book an Inspection") should be clearly the most prominent option. Too many competing CTAs create decision paralysis.
    Why it matters: When visitors see "Call Us," "Get a Quote," "Request Service," "Chat Now," and "Learn More" all equally prominent, they do nothing. One clear CTA consistently outperforms multiple competing options.

Trust & Social Proof

  • Checkpoint 63
    Homepage clearly states what you do, where you do it, and for whom
    Read your homepage headline and the first paragraph. Within 5 seconds of landing, a new visitor should know: what service you provide, which city/area you serve, and who your customers are. Test this with someone who has never seen your site.
    Why it matters: Visitors decide in 3–5 seconds whether they're in the right place. A vague headline like "Quality Service You Can Trust" tells them nothing. "Roof Replacement & Repair in Dallas, TX" tells them everything they need.
  • Checkpoint 64
    Customer reviews or testimonials appear on the homepage
    Confirm that at least 3–5 real customer testimonials (with first name, city, and preferably a photo) are visible on the homepage. Google review widgets that display live reviews are ideal.
    Why it matters: Social proof is the #1 trust factor for home services. Homeowners are inviting a stranger into their home — they need validation from other people who've already done it. Testimonials on the homepage can increase conversion rate by 30–50%.
  • Checkpoint 65
    Trust signals are visible — license number, insurance, certifications
    Check your homepage and footer for: state license number, "licensed & insured" badge, manufacturer certifications (GAF, Carrier, Trane, etc.), BBB accreditation, and years in business.
    Why it matters: For high-ticket home services, professional credentials reduce risk in the buyer's mind. A license number displayed prominently signals legitimacy that a new competitor without one cannot easily replicate.
  • Checkpoint 66
    Before/after photos are featured on the homepage or service pages
    Confirm at least one before/after photo section exists on your homepage or primary service page. This should be real job photography — not stock images or illustrations.
    Why it matters: Before/after photos are the most credible proof of your work quality. They are also the most browsed section on home service websites. Their absence is a missed conversion opportunity that almost every contractor's site can improve.

Analytics & Tracking

  • Checkpoint 67
    Google Analytics (GA4) is installed and tracking correctly
    Log into Google Analytics and confirm your website is showing active users in the Realtime report. If GA is not installed, visit your website and check that your analytics shows a session — if it does not, the tracking code is missing or broken.
    Why it matters: Without analytics, you're running a business with no financial reporting. You cannot know which pages convert, which traffic sources perform, or whether your website is improving over time.
  • Checkpoint 68
    Call tracking is set up — you know which sources drive phone calls
    Confirm you are using a call tracking solution — whether Google Ads call extensions (free), CallRail, CallTrackingMetrics, or a similar tool — to know which campaigns and channels are generating actual phone calls.
    Why it matters: For most home service businesses, the phone call IS the lead. Without call tracking, you cannot connect your marketing spend to actual business results. You're flying blind on your highest-value conversions.
  • Checkpoint 69
    Local pages exist targeting your key service cities by name
    Check if your website has pages specifically for each major city you serve — e.g., "/roof-replacement-dallas/" or "/hvac-repair-plano-tx/." Even 2–3 city pages can dramatically improve organic local search rankings.
    Why it matters: Google organic results and the Local Pack favor websites with location-specific content. A city landing page outranks a generic service page for local searches — and local organic traffic is free, high-intent traffic.
  • Checkpoint 70
    Form submissions send an auto-response email or text immediately
    Fill out your own contact form and confirm you receive an auto-confirmation message. The homeowner should also receive one — confirming receipt and setting expectations for when you'll call them back.
    Why it matters: Without an instant auto-response, homeowners who fill out a form have no confirmation their message was received. Many will call a competitor in the meantime. An instant "We got it — expect a call within the hour" message dramatically reduces lead bleed.
