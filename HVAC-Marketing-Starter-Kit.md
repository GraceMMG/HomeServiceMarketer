### Google Ads Pro Tips

**Targeting Recommendations:**
- Set a 25–40 mile radius around your service area
- Use "Phrase match" and "Exact match" keywords — avoid broad match until you have conversion data
- Add negative keywords: "DIY," "YouTube," "how to," "used," "free," "careers," "jobs"
- Schedule ads to run during your business hours plus 2 hours before open and 2 hours after close
- Set bid adjustments: +20% mobile, +15% for 7–9 AM and 5–8 PM (peak homeowner search times)

**Top Keywords to Target:**
- `[city] HVAC repair` | `AC not cooling` | `furnace repair near me`
- `air conditioner installation [city]` | `HVAC tune up` | `emergency AC repair`
- `new heating system cost` | `heat pump installation` | `central air installation`

## What to Post on Facebook

### Content Types That Work Best for HVAC on Facebook

**1. Educational Posts (35% of your content)**
Facebook users respond well to informative, helpful content. Write posts that answer common questions before customers even ask them.

- "Why does my AC smell musty when it first turns on?"
- "The difference between a heat pump and a traditional furnace"
- "How to read your thermostat's error codes"
- "What MERV rating air filter should you actually use?"
- Seasonal prep guides (spring startup, winter shutdown)

*Format tip: Use numbered lists or short paragraphs. Break up text with line spaces. Include one relevant image.*

**2. Customer Reviews & Testimonials (20% of your content)**
Screenshot 5-star Google or Facebook reviews and post them as images. Always tag the customer if possible and thank them by name. These posts build trust faster than any ad.

*Best practice: Ask for permission before using a photo. Offer a small incentive (discount on next service) for customers who leave a review and let you feature it.*

**3. Promotions & Offers (20% of your content)**
Facebook is a strong channel for driving direct bookings. Keep offers simple and time-limited.

- Monthly tune-up specials with countdown
- New customer discounts
- Referral bonuses ("Refer a friend, both get $25 off")
- Holiday promotions (Mother's Day, Labor Day, Back to School)
- Maintenance plan enrollment drives

*Format tip: Use a branded graphic with a clear price, what's included, and an expiration date.*

**4. Behind-the-Scenes & Team Content (15% of your content)**
People hire people, not companies. Show your team in action, your trucks, your warehouse, your office dog — whatever makes your business human.

- "Day in the life" posts
- New employee introductions
- Certifications and training milestones
- Company anniversary posts
- "We just hit [X] 5-star reviews!" celebrations

**5. Community & Local Content (10% of your content)**
- Sponsor a local little league team? Post about it.
- Participating in a neighborhood event? Document it.
- Weather alerts or local news relevant to home comfort

*Engagement tip: Ask local questions. "What's your go-to spot to cool off in [City]?" generates comments and shows the algorithm your page is active.*

## What to Post on Instagram

### Content Types That Work Best for HVAC on Instagram

**1. Reels (40% of your content — highest reach)**
Instagram prioritizes video. Even simple 15–30 second videos dramatically outperform static images in reach.

Reel ideas for HVAC:
- "3 things to check before you call for AC repair" (talking head + B-roll)
- "HVAC job in [X] seconds — time lapse of a full system install"
- "Before vs. after: 20-year-old system replacement"
- "Meet our team — 30-second version"
- "We found THIS inside a homeowner's ducts..." (satisfying/shocking reveal)
- "Signs your AC won't make it through summer" (list format with text overlay)

*Production tip: You don't need professional video. Use a phone mounted on a tripod, shoot in landscape or vertical, add text overlays using CapCut (free). Keep it authentic — raw footage performs well.*

**2. Before & After Photos (25% of your content)**
Instagram is visual. Before/after content is the highest-performing post type for home services. Show:

- Old, dirty equipment → new clean install
- Cluttered/damaged ductwork → professional repair
- Grimy coils before cleaning → spotless after
- Customer's uncomfortable expression in a hot house → smiling with new AC running

*Caption formula: "This [equipment type] was [X] years old and [describe the problem]. We replaced it with [new system] in [X] hours. The homeowner said [brief quote]. Comment if you want us to check your system!"*

**3. Carousels (20% of your content)**
Swipeable multi-image posts drive the most saves and shares on Instagram. Use carousels for:

- "5 signs your HVAC system needs replacing" (one sign per slide)
- "How a tune-up works — step by step" (one step per slide)
- "Comparing HVAC brands: Carrier vs. Trane vs. Lennox"
- "Summer HVAC prep checklist" (one item per slide)

*Design tip: Use Canva (free) with a consistent branded template. Keep each slide text minimal — 1 headline + 1–2 sentences max.*

**4. Team & Culture Posts (15% of your content)**
Instagram audiences respond strongly to authentic team content. Use Stories for casual behind-the-scenes. Use the Feed for more polished team highlights.

### Email 3: Educational / Problem-Solving (Send: 48 hours after Email 1, if no appointment booked)

**Subject Line:** Is your HVAC system trying to tell you something?

**Preview Text:** The 5 warning signs most homeowners miss until it's too late.

---

Hi [First Name],

Whether or not you end up choosing us, we want this email to be genuinely useful.

Here are the **5 early warning signs** that your HVAC system needs attention — signs that most homeowners dismiss until they become expensive emergencies:

**1. Your energy bills are creeping up**
A system that's losing efficiency will work harder to maintain temperature — and you'll see it on your utility bill. A tune-up can often restore 15–20% efficiency.

**2. Rooms are inconsistently heated or cooled**
Hot spots and cold spots throughout your home usually point to duct leaks, airflow restrictions, or a failing blower motor.

**3. It's making new noises**
Banging, grinding, squealing, or rattling noises are your system's way of waving a red flag. These sounds usually precede a component failure.

**4. Humidity feels "off"**
Your HVAC system plays a major role in home humidity. If your home feels sticky in summer or dry in winter, your system may not be performing correctly.

**5. The system is over 10 years old**
Not a reason to replace it — but it is a reason to have it inspected. Systems over 10–12 years old have significantly higher failure risk heading into peak season.

# SECTION 5: Local SEO Checklist for HVAC Companies

## Why Local SEO Matters
When someone searches "AC repair near me" or "HVAC company [City]," Google decides which businesses to show in the Local Pack (the map results) and organic listings based on three core factors: **Relevance**, **Distance**, and **Prominence**. This checklist covers all three.

---

## Part A: Google Business Profile (GBP)

This is the single most important local SEO asset you have. Treat it like a second website.

### Setup & Verification
- [ ] Claim and verify your Google Business Profile at `business.google.com`
- [ ] Verify via phone, text, or postcard (postcard takes 5–7 days)
- [ ] Set your primary business category to **"HVAC Contractor"**
- [ ] Add secondary categories: "Air Conditioning Contractor," "Heating Contractor," "Air Duct Cleaning Service" (as applicable)
- [ ] Set your service area — list every city, town, and zip code you serve
- [ ] Set your service radius if you don't have a physical storefront
- [ ] Add your verified business address (even if you're a mobile-only operation, you can hide the address while still setting a service area)

### Business Information
- [ ] Business name matches exactly what's on your license, website, and other directories
- [ ] Phone number is a local area code (not a toll-free number)
- [ ] Website URL is correct and goes directly to your homepage or a location page
- [ ] Business hours are accurate — including holiday hours
- [ ] Add "More hours" for emergency/24-hour service if applicable
- [ ] Business description is complete: 750 characters, includes primary keywords naturally (e.g., "[City] HVAC repair," "air conditioning installation," "heating service")

### Services
- [ ] Add every service you offer using Google's service menu
  - [ ] AC Repair
  - [ ] AC Installation
  - [ ] Furnace Repair
  - [ ] Furnace Installation
  - [ ] Heat Pump Installation
  - [ ] HVAC Maintenance / Tune-Up
  - [ ] Indoor Air Quality
  - [ ] Duct Cleaning
  - [ ] Thermostat Installation
  - [ ] Emergency HVAC Service
- [ ] Add pricing ranges where possible (helps set expectations and qualify leads)
- [ ] Add service descriptions for each service (2–3 sentences minimum)

### Photos & Videos
- [ ] Upload at least 10 photos (Google profiles with 10+ photos get 35% more click-throughs)
- [ ] Photo types to include:
  - [ ] Exterior of building or branded vehicles
  - [ ] Technicians at work (with customer permission)
  - [ ] Before/after installation and repair photos
  - [ ] Team photo
  - [ ] Any certifications or awards displayed on the wall
  - [ ] Logo (square format, 250x250 minimum)
  - [ ] Cover photo (1024x575 recommended)
- [ ] Add at least one video (30–60 seconds, showing your team or work)
- [ ] Set a profile photo (your logo or best team photo)

### Google Posts
- [ ] Publish at least 1 Google Post per week (offers, updates, events)
- [ ] Every post should include:
  - [ ] A compelling image
  - [ ] Clear offer or update (50–300 words)
  - [ ] A CTA button (Book, Call, Learn More)
- [ ] Seasonal offers perform especially well — schedule these in advance

### Reviews
- [ ] You have a direct Google Review link to share with customers (get it from your GBP dashboard)
- [ ] You ask every customer for a review via text or email after service
- [ ] You have a process for responding to every review — positive and negative — within 48 hours
- [ ] Target: 50+ reviews with an average of 4.5+ stars to be competitive in most markets
- [ ] Responding to reviews template:

*Positive:* "Thank you so much, [Name]! It was a pleasure working with you — we're so glad your [AC/furnace] is running like new. We hope to be your go-to HVAC team for years to come!"

*Negative:* "Hi [Name], thank you for your honest feedback. We take every concern seriously and would love the opportunity to make this right. Please call us at [Phone] or email [Email] so we can resolve this directly. We're committed to your satisfaction."

## Part B: Website Local SEO

### On-Page Optimization
- [ ] Your homepage title tag includes your primary keyword and city: `HVAC Repair & Installation in [City, State] | [Company Name]`
- [ ] Meta description includes city, service, and a CTA (150–160 characters)
- [ ] H1 heading on homepage includes city + service (e.g., "Trusted HVAC Services in [City, State]")
- [ ] NAP (Name, Address, Phone) displayed in the footer on every page — matches GBP exactly
- [ ] Phone number is click-to-call (HTML: `<a href="tel:+1XXXXXXXXXX">`)
- [ ] Your service area is clearly described in text on the homepage and service pages
- [ ] You have a dedicated page for each major service (AC Repair, AC Installation, Furnace Repair, etc.)
- [ ] Each service page has 300+ words of unique content
- [ ] You have a dedicated page for each major city/area you serve

### Location Pages (If You Serve Multiple Cities)
For each city where you want to rank, create a unique page with:
- [ ] URL: `yoursite.com/hvac-[city-name]`
- [ ] Unique H1: "HVAC Services in [City Name], [State]"
- [ ] 300–500 words of content that mentions the city name naturally 3–5 times
- [ ] Local landmarks, neighborhoods, or context that prove you serve that area
- [ ] Embedded Google Map of the city/area
- [ ] Unique testimonial from a customer in that city (if available)
- [ ] Same CTA and contact info as all other pages

### Technical SEO
- [ ] Site loads in under 3 seconds (test at `PageSpeed.web.dev`)
- [ ] Site is mobile-friendly (test at `search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly`)
- [ ] Site has an SSL certificate (URL starts with `https://`)
- [ ] XML sitemap exists and is submitted to Google Search Console
- [ ] Google Search Console is set up and verified
- [ ] Google Analytics (GA4) is installed and tracking

### Schema Markup
- [ ] `LocalBusiness` schema installed (or `HVACBusiness` if available)
- [ ] Schema includes: business name, address, phone, hours, service area, geo coordinates, review aggregate
- [ ] Use Google's Structured Data Testing Tool to verify
- [ ] Add `FAQPage` schema to pages with FAQ sections

Citations are online mentions of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP). Consistent citations across the web strengthen your local ranking signals.

- [ ] **Nextdoor** — `nextdoor.com/business`

## Part D: Link Building for HVAC

Links from other websites to yours remain a powerful local ranking signal. Focus on quality over quantity.

- [ ] **Manufacturer Partner Pages** — Carrier, Trane, Lennox, and other brands have dealer locators. If you're a certified dealer, get listed on their website with a link back to yours.
- [ ] **Local Chamber of Commerce** — Most chambers offer member directory listings with links
- [ ] **Local Newspaper / News Sites** — Pitch a seasonal tips story to your local newspaper. HVAC tips for summer/winter are evergreen local interest content.
- [ ] **Supplier Links** — Ask your HVAC parts suppliers if they have contractor directories
- [ ] **Sponsor Local Organizations** — Little leagues, school events, and charity organizations often list sponsors with links
- [ ] **Home Builder / Contractor Networks** — Partner with local builders, plumbers, and electricians for reciprocal referrals and links
- [ ] **HVAC Blog Content** — Publish one blog post per month answering a common customer question. Well-written content attracts natural links over time.
