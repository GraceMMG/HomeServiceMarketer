  • Bid adjustments: +20% mobile, +15% for 7–9 AM and 5–8 PM

What to Post on Facebook

1. Educational Posts — 35%

Answer common questions before customers ask them:

  • "Why does my AC smell musty?"
  • "Heat pump vs. traditional furnace"
  • "How to read thermostat error codes"
  • "What MERV rating filter should you use?"
  • Seasonal prep guides (spring startup, winter shutdown)

Use numbered lists or short paragraphs. Include one relevant image.

2. Customer Reviews — 20%

Screenshot 5-star Google or Facebook reviews and post them as images. Always tag the customer if possible and thank them by name.

Ask for permission before using a photo. Offer a small incentive for customers who let you feature their review.

3. Promotions & Offers — 20%
  • Monthly tune-up specials with countdown
  • Referral bonuses ("Refer a friend, both get $25 off")
  • Holiday promotions
  • Maintenance plan enrollment drives
4. Behind-the-Scenes — 15%

People hire people, not companies. Show your team in action.

  • "Day in the life" posts
  • New employee introductions
  • Certifications and training milestones
  • Company anniversary posts
  • "We just hit [X] 5-star reviews!" celebrations
5. Community & Local — 10%
  • Sponsor a local little league team? Post about it.
  • Participating in a neighborhood event? Document it.
  • Weather alerts relevant to home comfort

Ask local questions. "What's your go-to spot to cool off in [City]?" generates comments and signals to the algorithm that your page is active.

Facebook Post Anatomy Template

Copy this structure for every Facebook post
[HOOK] — Bold opening line that creates curiosity or states a benefit
[BODY] — 2–4 short paragraphs or a bulleted list. Educate, inform, or offer.
[SOCIAL PROOF] — Optional: "Our customers love this because..." or a brief quote
[CTA] — One clear action: Call, book, comment, tag someone, click the link
[LINK] — Add your booking or service page link in the post body, not just the comments

What to Post on Instagram

1. Reels — 40% (Highest Reach)

Instagram prioritizes video. Even 15–30 second clips dramatically outperform static images.

Reel ideas for HVAC:

  • "3 things to check before you call for AC repair" (talking head + B-roll)
  • "HVAC job in [X] seconds — time lapse of a full system install"
  • "Before vs. after: 20-year-old system replacement"
  • "Meet our team — 30-second version"
  • "We found THIS inside a homeowner's ducts..."
  • "Signs your AC won't make it through summer"

No professional video needed. Phone + tripod + CapCut (free). Raw footage performs well.

2. Before & After Photos — 25%

Highest-performing post type for home services. Show:

  • Old dirty equipment → new clean install
  • Cluttered/damaged ductwork → professional repair
  • Grimy coils → spotless after cleaning

Caption: "This [equipment] was [X] years old and [describe problem]. We replaced it with [new system] in [X] hours."

3. Carousels — 20%
  • "5 signs your HVAC system needs replacing" (one sign per slide)
  • "How a tune-up works — step by step"
  • "Comparing HVAC brands: Carrier vs. Trane vs. Lennox"

Use Canva (free). Keep each slide to 1 headline + 1–2 sentences max.

Instagram Story Ideas (3–5 per week)

Stories disappear after 24 hours — use them for timely, casual content
  • Morning: "We're heading out to [neighborhood] today! Anyone need service?"
  • Poll: "How often do you change your air filter?" (Every month / Every 3 months / Honestly... never)
  • Quiz: "True or false: Running your AC constantly uses more energy than setting it to 78°F"
  • Behind the scenes: Techs loading the truck, driving to a job, finishing an install
  • Limited-time offer: "24-hour flash deal — DM us for $30 off"
  • Customer shoutout: "Thank you [customer] for the kind words!"

Pro tip: Add the "Book Now" or link sticker to every story that includes a promotion.

Local SEO Checklist for HVAC Companies
Everything you need to rank in Google's Local Pack and organic results — covering all three core ranking factors.
Why Local SEO Matters

When someone searches "AC repair near me," Google decides which businesses to show based on three core factors: Relevance, Distance, and Prominence. This checklist covers all three.

Part A: Google Business Profile (GBP)

This is the single most important local SEO asset you have. Treat it like a second website.

Setup & Verification
  • Claim and verify your Google Business Profile at business.google.com
  • Set your primary category to "HVAC Contractor"
  • Add secondary categories: "Air Conditioning Contractor," "Heating Contractor," "Air Duct Cleaning Service"
  • Set your service area — every city, town, and zip code you serve
  • Add your verified business address
Business Information
  • Business name matches exactly what's on your license and website
  • Phone number is a local area code (not toll-free)
  • Website URL is correct
  • Business hours are accurate — including holiday hours
  • Business description: 750 characters, includes primary keywords naturally
Services to Add in GBP
  • AC Repair
  • AC Installation
  • Furnace Repair
  • Furnace Installation
  • Heat Pump Installation
  • HVAC Maintenance / Tune-Up
  • Indoor Air Quality
  • Duct Cleaning
  • Thermostat Installation
  • Emergency HVAC Service
  • Add pricing ranges where possible
  • Add service descriptions (2–3 sentences each)
Photos & Videos
  • Upload at least 10 photos (profiles with 10+ photos get 35% more click-throughs)
  • Exterior of building or branded vehicles
  • Technicians at work (with customer permission)
  • Before/after installation and repair photos
  • Team photo
  • Logo (square format, 250×250 minimum)
  • Cover photo (1024×575 recommended)
  • At least one video (30–60 seconds)
Reviews
  • You have a direct Google Review link to share with customers
  • You ask every customer for a review via text or email after service

Part D: Link Building for HVAC

Links from other websites remain a powerful local ranking signal. Focus on quality over quantity.

Link Building Opportunities
  • Manufacturer Partner Pages — Carrier, Trane, Lennox, and other brands have dealer locators. If you're a certified dealer, get listed on their website with a link back to yours.
  • Local Chamber of Commerce — Most chambers offer member directory listings with links.
  • Local Newspaper / News Sites — Pitch a seasonal tips story. HVAC tips for summer/winter are evergreen local interest content.
  • Supplier Links — Ask your HVAC parts suppliers if they have contractor directories.
  • Sponsor Local Organizations — Little leagues, school events, and charity organizations often list sponsors with links.
  • Home Builder / Contractor Networks — Partner with local builders, plumbers, and electricians for reciprocal referrals and links.
  • HVAC Blog Content — Publish one blog post per month answering a common customer question. Well-written content attracts natural links over time.
