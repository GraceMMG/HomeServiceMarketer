Before/After Photo Strategy Guide
Before/after photos are the highest-performing roofing content on every platform. Here's exactly how to capture, edit, and deploy them.
Why Before/After Photos Work for Roofing

Roofing is a high-trust, high-cost purchase. Before/after photos do the selling for you — they prove your work, demonstrate the transformation, and trigger the thought "I need that for my roof too." A single great before/after photo can generate dozens of leads when boosted on Facebook or posted to Google Business Profile.

Equipment You Need (Budget: $0–$150)

Minimum Setup (Free)
  • Your phone camera — Any iPhone 12+ or modern Android is sufficient
  • Natural lighting — Shoot in early morning or late afternoon (golden hour) for best results
  • Clean lens — Wipe it before every shoot. Dust kills photo quality.
  • Level shots — Keep the horizon straight. Crooked photos look unprofessional.
Upgraded Setup ($50–$150)
  • Phone tripod/mount (~$20) — Eliminates shake on ground-level shots
  • DJI Mini drone (~$299, optional) — Aerial before/after shots are exceptional for social ads
  • Wide-angle lens clip (~$15) — Shows more of the roof in each shot
  • Canva Pro (~$13/mo) — Add your logo, brand colors, and overlays to every photo

The 5-Shot System — What to Capture on Every Job

Train every crew to take these 5 photos on every job. It takes under 5 minutes and creates a library of marketing content automatically.

Shot 1: The "Before" Wide Shot
  • Stand at street level or across the yard — capture the entire roofline
  • Shoot from the same angle you'll use for the "after" shot
  • If there's visible damage (missing shingles, sagging), get close enough to show it clearly
  • Shoot in landscape (horizontal) orientation for ads, portrait for Instagram stories/Reels
Shot 2: The Damage Close-Up
  • Get 2–3 feet from the damage — hail dents, cracked/missing shingles, lifted flashing, soft spots
  • This shot creates urgency and shows why the job needed to be done
  • If using drone footage, capture the full roof with visible damage from above
Shot 3: Mid-Job Progress
  • Capture the crew working — action shots build trust and show professionalism
  • Include a wide shot showing the crew on the roof with ladders and materials visible
  • Short 15-second video clips work even better than still photos for Reels/Stories
Shot 4: The "After" Wide Shot
  • Stand in the exact same spot as Shot 1 — same angle, same framing
  • Shoot from street level for maximum visual impact showing the whole house
  • Capture during the same time of day if possible for consistent lighting
  • Include the full house — gutters, siding, landscaping — to show how clean you left it
Shot 5: The Detail "After" Shot
  • Close-up of the finished shingles, flashing, ridge cap, or ventilation
  • Shows craftsmanship — this is what separates you from cheap competitors
  • Get the crew's hands and tools in frame for authenticity

Editing Your Photos (Free Tools)

Google Photos / Apple Photos (Free)
  • Brightness +15–20 to show detail on dark shingles
  • Contrast +10 to make colors pop
  • Shadows +20 to reveal detail in dark areas
  • Crop to remove distractions at edges
  • Straighten if the horizon is tilted
Canva (Free/Pro) — For Social Posts
  • Use the "Side by Side" template for before/after posts
  • Add your logo in the top corner (30–40px, semi-transparent)
  • Add a text overlay: "Before → After" in your brand colors
  • Export at highest quality for ads (use PNG for best quality)
  • Create a template you can reuse on every job

Google Business Profile Optimization Guide
Your GBP is your single most powerful free marketing tool. Roofing contractors with fully optimized profiles get 3–5x more calls from local search.
Why GBP Matters More Than Your Website

When someone searches "roofer near me" or "roof replacement [City]," Google shows the Local Pack — the map with 3 businesses. The contractor in that pack gets 60–80% of the clicks. Your GBP ranking determines whether you appear. This guide will get you into that pack and keep you there.

Step 1: Claim & Verify Your Profile

Profile Setup Checklist
  • Go to business.google.com — claim your listing if you haven't already
  • Verify by postcard, phone, or video (video verification is now common — show your truck, tools, and worksite)
  • Business name: Use your exact legal/DBA name — no keyword stuffing (e.g., NOT "Best Roofer [City] LLC")
  • Primary category: Roofing Contractor
  • Secondary categories: Add "Roofing Supply Store," "Gutter Cleaning Service," "Siding Contractor" if applicable
  • Service area: Add every city, town, and zip code you serve — up to 20 locations
  • Phone number: Use a local number (not toll-free). Trackable call numbers are fine.
  • Website: Link to your homepage or a dedicated roofing landing page
  • Hours: Set accurate hours. If you offer 24/7 emergency service, indicate that in your description.

Step 2: Write a Description That Converts

Your GBP description is 750 characters. Most contractors waste it with generic filler. Here's a template that includes local keywords and trust signals:

GBP Description Template
[Company Name] is [City]'s trusted roofing contractor, serving [City], [City 2], and [City 3] since [Year]. We specialize in roof replacement, storm damage repair, hail damage inspections, and new roof installation for residential homeowners.

Licensed and insured in [State] (Lic. #[Number]). [X]+ 5-star Google reviews. We work directly with all major insurance companies and offer flexible financing options.

Call [Phone Number] for a free inspection or estimate. Same-week appointments available.

Step 3: Photos — The Ranking Factor Most Roofers Ignore

Google ranks GBP profiles with more photos higher — and customers click profiles with more photos more often. Most roofing contractors have 5–10 photos. Get to 50+ and you'll stand out dramatically.

What to Upload (Priority Order)
  1. Before/after photos of completed jobs (use your 5-Shot System)
  2. Your crew working on a roof
  3. Your branded truck(s) at a job site
  4. Close-ups of quality finished work (ridge caps, flashing, valleys)
  5. Your team photo or crew headshots
  6. Photos of your office or warehouse (if applicable)
  7. Screenshots of 5-star Google reviews (yes, upload these)
Photo Upload Rules
  • Upload at least 2 new photos every week — fresh photos signal an active business
  • Name your photo files before uploading: "roof-replacement-dallas-texas.jpg" not "IMG_3847.jpg"
  • Minimum resolution: 720×720px. Bigger is better.
  • Never use stock photos — Google can detect them and they don't build trust
  • Also encourage customers to upload their own photos after a job

Step 4: Google Review Generation System

The Review Multiplier Effect

Google ranks businesses with more reviews and higher ratings higher in local search. Every new 5-star review also increases your click-through rate from the map pack. A contractor with 150 reviews gets roughly 3–4x more profile clicks than one with 25 reviews at the same rating.

The 3-Touch Review Request System
  • Touch 1 — Verbal ask at job completion: "We really appreciate your business. If you're happy with the work, would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It helps our family business a lot." Then immediately text them the link.
  • Touch 2 — Text message (same day): "[Customer Name], thanks for trusting us with your roof! If you have 2 minutes, we'd love a Google review: [your Google review short link]. Thank you! — [Your Name], [Company]"
  • Touch 3 — Follow-up text (3 days later): "Hi [Name], just following up — did everything look good after the crew left? If you have a minute to leave us a review it would mean a lot: [link]"

How to Get Your Google Review Link

In your Google Business Profile dashboard, go to Home → Get more reviews → Share review form. Copy that short link and save it. This is the link you text every customer.

Step 5: GBP Posts — Post Weekly

What to Post Weekly
  • Before/after photo from a recent job
  • Seasonal tip (spring: pre-storm inspection; fall: pre-winter check)
  • Current offer or promotion
  • Completed job highlight with city name in caption

GBP Post Best Practices
  • Include your city name in every post ("Just finished a roof in [Neighborhood], [City]")

  • Add a CTA button: "Call Now" or "Book Online"
  • Posts expire after 7 days — set a weekly reminder to post
  • Use a scheduling tool (Buffer, Publer) if you want to batch-create posts

Storm Season Marketing Playbook
How to deploy marketing within 24–48 hours of a major storm event and capture emergency roofing leads before your competitors wake up.
Speed Is Everything After a Storm

In storm response marketing, the first contractor to reach homeowners wins the lion's share of jobs. Within 24 hours of a major hail or wind event, homeowners are actively searching, scrolling, and calling. The window of maximum urgency lasts 48–72 hours. This playbook is designed to get your marketing live within hours, not days.

Before a Storm (Prepare in Advance)

Pre-Storm Setup Checklist — Do These Today
  • Save Ad #1 (Storm Damage) from Section 1 in Meta Ads Manager as a draft — ready to activate with one click
  • Set up a "Storm Damage Inspection" landing page on your website with a simple form (name, phone, address, message)
  • Install the Meta Pixel on your website so you can retarget storm visitors
  • Create a text message template for storm outreach to your existing customer list
  • Set up a Google Local Services Ad account (takes 1–2 weeks to verify — do this now)
  • Prepare door hanger template (Canva) with storm damage messaging — can be printed in hours at local print shop
  • Sign up for storm alert services (StormAware, Hail Trace, or simply follow local weather on Twitter/X)

The 48-Hour Storm Response Timeline

0–4 Hours After Storm
Activate Your Paid Ads Immediately
  • Enable your pre-saved Facebook/Instagram storm damage ad (Section 1, Ad #1)
  • Set budget to $50–$100/day for the next 72 hours
  • Post on your Facebook Business Page: "We're dispatching crews across [City] following last night's storm. Free inspections — call or DM us to get on the schedule."
  • Post the same message to your personal Facebook profile and ask employees to share
  • Update your Google Business Profile: Add a post about storm response
4–12 Hours After Storm
Activate Google & Text Marketing
  • If you have Google Local Services Ads set up, increase your budget and confirm your availability for the week
  • Create a Google Search Ad targeting "storm damage roof [city]," "hail damage roof repair [city]," "emergency roofer [city]"
  • Text your existing customer list: "Hi [Name], [Company Name] here. We're reaching out to [City] customers following last night's storm. Free damage inspections available this week — reply YES if you'd like us to check your roof."
12–24 Hours After Storm
Ground Game — Canvassing & Door Hangers
  • Deploy crews to the hardest-hit neighborhoods for door-to-door inspections (with homeowner permission)
  • Knock on doors within 2–3 blocks of every job you complete this week — neighbors are watching
  • Leave door hangers on every home on every street you visit ("We just inspected your neighbor's roof at [address] — complimentary inspection available")
  • Take street-level photos of visibly damaged roofs to use in ads (do NOT go on any roof without homeowner permission)
24–48 Hours After Storm
Follow Up & Scale What's Working
  • Call every lead from the first 24 hours within 5 minutes of them submitting a form (leads go cold fast)
  • Check your Facebook ad results — increase budget on any ad with a low cost-per-lead
  • Post your first before/after photo from a storm job — these spread organically in storm-affected communities
  • Boost the before/after post for $10–20/day targeting the affected zip codes
  • Follow up with any homeowners who said "maybe" or "call me next week" — their urgency is highest right now

2
Education Email — What Homeowners Need to Know
Send Day 2
Subject:5 signs your roof needs replacing (most homeowners miss #3)
Preview:Before our inspection, know what to look for...

Hi [First Name],

We're looking forward to your inspection. Before we come out, we wanted to share the 5 most common signs of roof damage that homeowners miss — because understanding what we're looking for helps you ask better questions when we're there.

1. Granules in Your Gutters or Downspouts
Asphalt shingles have a granular coating that protects against UV and weather. When shingles age or suffer impact damage, granules shed into gutters. If you clean your gutters and find grit or debris that looks like coarse sand, that's a red flag.

2. Curling or Cupping Shingles
Shingles that are curling up at the edges (cupping) or buckling in the middle (clawing) are at the end of their lifespan. This happens from moisture trapped beneath the shingle layer.

3. Dark Streaks or Staining
Black streaks along the roof surface are usually algae or moss growth — a sign of moisture retention that accelerates shingle breakdown. Some homeowners confuse this with dirt.

If you've noticed any brown or yellow staining on your ceilings, there's already moisture penetrating the roof system. Don't wait — water damage spreads fast.
